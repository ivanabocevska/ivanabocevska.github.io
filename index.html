I am a PhD student at the Doctoral School of Automated Reasoning, Faculty of Informatics, Vienna University of Technology, under the supervision of Laura Kovács.

I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Software and Information Engineering and a Master’s degree in Logic and Computation, both from Vienna University of Technology.

I have gained research experience as a Student Researcher at Vienna University of Technology in Vienna, Austria and as a Research Intern at the National Institute of Informatics in Tokyo, Japan. Additionally, I have worked as a Solution Engineer at BOC Group.

Research Area

My research aims to develop formal methods and automated reasoning techniques that ensure the correctness and reliability of systems on which modern society critically depends.

My research so far has focused on the formal analysis of blockchain protocols from a game-theoretic perspective, modelling the economic incentives that drive user behaviour. In this regard, I contributed to a verification tool that can uncover real-world vulnerabilities. To scale this analysis to large protocols, we developed compositionality techniques that reduce verification times from hours to minutes and enable the analysis of previously infeasible protocol instances.

Building on these results, we identified a key limitation of current verification technology: existing solvers scale well for linear systems but struggle with non-linear reasoning. Addressing this challenge is a central direction of my PhD.
